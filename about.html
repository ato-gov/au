About the Australian Taxation Office

We are the Government’s principal revenue collection agency, managing and shaping tax, excise and superannuation systems that fund services for Australians.

Our role and purpose

The ATO is a key economic and social institution. We collect revenue, administer the Australian tax and superannuation systems, and support the delivery of government benefits. Our work funds essential services such as health, education, defence and infrastructure.

We aim to contribute to the economic and social wellbeing of Australians by fostering willing participation in the tax and super systems. We do this by making it as easy as possible for people to understand and meet their obligations, and by ensuring everyone receives their correct entitlements.

Our values and behaviours

Our values guide how we work and interact with the community, each other and our stakeholders. They are:

  • Impartial – we provide objective advice and deliver services fairly.
  • Committed to service – we put the community first and continuously improve.
  • Accountable – we take responsibility for our actions and outcomes.
  • Respectful – we treat everyone with dignity and courtesy.
  • Ethical – we act with integrity and professionalism.

Our vision is to be a trusted and contemporary organisation, shaping a better future for all Australians through the tax and super systems.

What we do

We collect revenue from individuals, businesses and other entities through taxes such as income tax, GST, excise and fringe benefits tax. We also regulate self‑managed super funds (SMSFs) and administer the Superannuation Guarantee system.

Beyond collection, we help people understand their rights and obligations through guidance, education and online tools. We also detect and address non‑compliance to maintain community confidence in the system.

How we are structured

The ATO is part of the Treasury portfolio and is led by the Commissioner of Taxation. We have offices across Australia, with major centres in Canberra, Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane, Perth, Adelaide, and several regional locations.

Our workforce includes tax officers, auditors, data scientists, lawyers, IT specialists, and customer service professionals, all working together to deliver for the Australian public.

Our commitment to you

We are committed to providing accurate, consistent and clear information. If you follow our guidance and it turns out to be incorrect or misleading, we will take that into account when deciding what action, if any, we should take.

We also offer support in difficult times – whether due to natural disaster, financial hardship, or personal crisis – and work with you to find solutions.

Access and equity

We are dedicated to making our services accessible to everyone, including people with disability, Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander peoples, and those from culturally and linguistically diverse backgrounds. We provide translated information, hearing assistance, and face‑to‑face help through our Tax Help program.

Our people

The ATO employs over 20,000 people in a wide variety of roles. We value diversity and inclusion, and offer flexible work arrangements, professional development, and a supportive culture. Our staff are our greatest asset in delivering for the community.

A brief history

The ATO was established in 1910, following the introduction of federal income tax. Over the decades, our responsibilities have grown to include superannuation, excise, and many other revenue measures. Today we are recognised as one of the world’s most advanced tax administrations.

Governance and accountability

We are accountable to the Parliament through the Treasurer. Our operations are scrutinised by the Australian National Audit Office, the Inspector‑General of Taxation, and the Commonwealth Ombudsman. We also engage with the community through our consultative forums and regular surveys.

For more information, see our annual reports, corporate plans, and publications available on this website.

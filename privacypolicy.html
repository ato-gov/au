About our privacy policy

Our privacy policy seeks to:

  • communicate our personal information handling practices
  • enhance the transparency of our operations
  • give individuals a better and more complete understanding of the personal information we hold and the way in which we deal with that information.

We review our privacy policy regularly and publish it on our website. If you would like to access a copy of our privacy policy in another form, or have feedback on our privacy policy, you can contact us.

The Privacy Act

The Privacy Act 1988 (Privacy Act) protects personal information and requires that we comply with the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs) in our handling of personal information.

Under the Privacy Act, ‘personal information’ means 'information or an opinion about an identified individual, or an individual who is reasonably identifiable, whether the information is true or not and whether the information is recorded in a material form or not'.

We also comply with the requirements of the Privacy (Australian Government Agencies – Governance) APP Code 2017 (APP code). Under the APP code, we have a Privacy Champion and a Privacy Officer, and undertake privacy impact assessments (PIAs).

Our undertaking to you

We undertake to collect personal information about you in a fair and lawful way and in a manner that is not unreasonably intrusive. We will be transparent and open about what personal information we collect, hold, use and disclose, as well as how you can make a privacy complaint.

Why we collect, hold, use and disclose personal information

In administering the tax and superannuation laws, we collect, hold, use and disclose a wide range of personal information. We have detailed privacy notices for every tax and super collection. We also collect, hold, use and disclose personal information in relation to our other functions, including as custodian of the Australian Business Register, for data matching, for prosecution and law enforcement, to verify your identity, as lead agency for Digital ID, in procuring goods and services, and as an employer.

Personal information we collect, hold, use and disclose

We collect, hold, use and disclose personal information about individuals that is necessary for or related to the administration of tax and super laws and other programs we administer.

Personal information includes names, addresses, emails, phone numbers, dates of birth, occupations, TFNs, ABNs, director IDs, bank account details, income, shareholdings, student numbers, trustee details, health fund details, AUSTRAC reports, business ownership, property sales data, and more.

We collect, hold, use and disclose personal information about super guarantee obligations, self-managed super funds, lost members, super co-contributions, and unclaimed super money.

TFNs are unique identifiers issued by us. They are protected by sections 8WA and 8WB of the Taxation Administration Act 1953 and the Privacy (Tax File Number) Rule 2015. We handle TFN information in accordance with those laws.

We participate in Digital ID and deliver myID and RAM. Personal information collected includes name, date of birth, address, email, identity document details. For more, see myID and RAM privacy policies.

We maintain a voiceprint biometric database including call recordings, personal information, and metadata. Voiceprints are created with your consent or in some cases to deal with unauthorised access. You can request deletion.

We keep records of registered tax agents including name, business name, contact details, registration number, ABN, email, bank details, and client lodgment history.

We collect personal information in personnel records for the purpose of discharging the Commissioner's employer powers under the Public Service Act 1999.

Forms support your interactions with us. Mandatory fields are clearly indicated. Any additional information you provide is at your discretion.

When you visit ato.gov.au we collect your server address, operating system, pages visited, and browser type. No attempt is made to identify you. Cookies are used to improve your experience. You can disable cookies, but this may affect functionality.

The ATO app includes myDeductions. Android users can back up to Google Drive. We do not access, collect, use, store or share personal information you input into myDeductions or your Google Drive.

How we collect personal information

We collect personal information directly from you, from other persons acting on your behalf, and from third parties including other government agencies, employers, and publicly available sources. We may also use formal information-gathering powers.

When we ask a third party for information about you

Tax and super laws allow us to obtain information from other parties. We normally tell you before seeking it, but there are circumstances where it may not be practicable, such as during audits or when collecting information from financial institutions, government agencies, or share registries.

How we hold personal information

We take steps to ensure your information is accurate, up‑to‑date and complete. We protect it against misuse, interference, loss, and unauthorised access using industry‑best security methods, including IT security audits, penetration testing, and risk management.

When you contact us

We must verify your identity before discussing your affairs. You can prove your identity by providing your date of birth, address, or details from an ATO‑generated notice. For general enquiries, you can deal with us without identifying yourself.

When we contact you

You have the right to be told why we are asking for your information and what legal authority we rely on.

Data matching and data exchange

We do checks to test compliance, including audit and verification programs and data matching. Data matching helps us identify non‑compliance and detect fraud. We acquire data from banks, financial institutions, employers, and welfare agencies, and match it with our own information. Detailed rules and voluntary guidelines protect your privacy.

Disclosures authorised by law

The law prohibits tax officers from disclosing taxpayer information except in specified circumstances, such as in the course of performing duties, to Commonwealth, state and territory entities for government purposes, to Ministers, and for law enforcement. See Procedures for disclosing protected information.

Disclosing to advisers, contractors and outsourcing

We may engage external advisers and contractors (e.g., call centres, form processors). They are bound by privacy and taxpayer confidentiality provisions, and contracts ensure your information is protected.

How we dispose of personal information

Personal information is treated as a Commonwealth record and retained in accordance with the Archives Act 1983 and records authorities issued by the National Archives. Disposal follows legal requirements.

Disclosing personal information to overseas recipients

We provide personal information to overseas recipients in accordance with international tax treaties and tax information exchange agreements (TIEAs) to combat tax evasion and ensure compliance. See Appendix 2 for a list of countries and jurisdictions.

How you can access your personal information and seek its correction

You can update your own personal information via our online services. You can access copies of your tax records via myGov. Your authorised representative can also access information via Online services for agents.

For documents not available online, you can request access under APP 12 or the Freedom of Information Act 1982. We will respond within 30 days. We do not charge for access requests.

You can also request correction of your personal information under APP 13. We will respond within 30 days.

What to do about a suspected breach of privacy

If you think your privacy has been breached, you can contact our Privacy Hotline on 1300 661 542 or lodge a formal complaint. We aim to contact you within 3 working days.

Appendix 1 – What information we collect, hold, use and disclose, and why

Includes Australian Business Register records, director IDs, prosecution and law enforcement records, prescribed taskforces, excise records, databases and data matching records, call recordings, vendor records, and employee records.
